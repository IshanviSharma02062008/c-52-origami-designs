Camel

Interesting Facts about Camel

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  2. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
  3. Camel's foot consists of two toes that spread when animal touches the ground and prevents sinking in the sand.

Chameleon

Interesting Facts about Chameleon

  • Chameleons spend most of their lives in bushes or trees, where they can hide from predators and wait for food.
  • They're unique among lizards because they have zygodactylous toes, meaning their feet can grip tree trunks and limbs.
  • Chameleons can often have over 20 babies in one period (called a clutch). Talk about a full house! Once a baby chameleon breaks through the membrane and shell of the egg, it is out in the world to start its life!

Pigeon

Interesting Facts about Pigeon

  • Pigeons can move their wings ten times per second and maintain heartbeats at the rate of 600 times per minute, during the period of 16 hours.
  • Pigeons can fly at the speed of 50 to 60 miles per hour. Fastest known pigeon managed to reach speed on 92 miles per hour.

Bear

Interesting Facts about bears

  • Bear skulls have unique adaptations to help eat a wide range of food.
  • They have large canine teeth to aid in opening logs to look for insects, and to capture and eat prey and occasionally eat the remains of animals that have died.
  • Considered by many wildlife biologists to be one of the most intelligent land animals of North America, bears possess the largest and most convoluted brains relative to their size of any land mammal. In the animal kingdom, their intelligence compares with that of higher primates.

Panda

Interesting Facts about Pandas

  • Another finding suggests pandas have remarkable memories, at least when it comes to their favorite food, bamboo.
  • Pandas have core areas where they prefer to feed, moving from one spot to the next as they exhaust the bamboo.
  • They are born furless and blind. The limbs of newborn pandas are so weak that they are not able to stand at all. For 2 months after birth, baby pandas rely on the mother's warmth, milk, and protection.

Flying Cicada

Interesting Facts about Flying Cicadas

  • Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs. They are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging.
  • They improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil. When they die, their rotting bodies put nitrogen into the soil.
  • Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes.
